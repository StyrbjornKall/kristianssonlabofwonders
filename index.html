Two million SEK from the Swedish Research Council to evaluate AI-based diagnostic methods in healthcare

The Swedish Research Council has decided which applications have been awarded grants for proof of concept in the field of infection in 2024. Erik Kristiansson and Anna Johnning from Mathematical Sciences in Gothenburg have been awarded grants for AI-based diagnostic methods in healthcare.

Swedish Radio highlights diagnostic methods in AI

Anna Johnning, Erik Kristiansson and colleagues are researching new ways to detect and map resistant bacteria and prevent their spread. On Monday this week, Anna Johnning was interviewed by Swedish Radio P4. Listen to the feature from minute 24:00.

International interest in AI method that detects toxic chemicals

Erik Kristiansson and Mikael Gustavsson, at Mathematical Sciences, have together with colleagues developed an AI method that can identify toxic chemicals, based solely on knowledge of the molecular structure. The results have been reported by, among others, American MSN, Japanese Note and German Chemie.

Erik Kristansson on Highly Cited Researchers list

​Three researchers from Chalmers can be found on Clarivate's list of the world's most cited in 2024, one of them is Erik Kristiansson in the division of applied mathematics and statistics.

Economic interests cloud hazardous chemicals reductions

In a recently published study Mikael Gustavsson and Erik Kristiansson have worked together with Jessica Coria at the Scool of Business, Economics and Law at University of Gothenburg. They have analysed different data sets regarding EU:s regulation of hazardous chemicals and the results show that economic interest has played a large role in shaping the regulations.
